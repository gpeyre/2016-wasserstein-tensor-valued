\caption{%%%
\textbf{Top row:} given two input fields of PSD matrices (displayed at time $t \in \{0,1\}$ using ellipses) on some domain (here a surface mesh), our Quantum Optimal Transport (Q-OT) method defines a continuous interpolating paths for $t \in [0,1]$. On contrary to usual linear interpolation schemes, Q-OT transport the ``mass'' of the tensors (size of the ellipses) as well are their anisotropy and orientation. This interpolation, and its extension to barycenter of several input fields, is computed using a fast extension of the well-known Sinkhorn algorithm.

\textbf{Bottom row:} an example application is the generation of a spatially varying anisotropic texture that can be used as a bump map to offset the surface in the normal direction.
